Precious Gifts from Mother Nature

Theruni Abeysinghe

Let's ask ourselves, who is Mother Nature?


Mother Nature is all the trees, bushes, plants animals and water that surround us in everyday life. All of these things are important and without them no living beings who live on this planet would be able to survive, but the most important things are all the trees. Let us now take a look at the numerous ways they help us.

The first, and most important gift we receive from trees is Oxygen. And oxygen equals life. Trees use a unique chemical process to turn light energy from the sun into this all important gas that provides all of us with life. This process is known as photosynthesis. They do this by mixing carbon dioxide and water together. Thus producing oxygen. They use a green pigment called chlorophyll to produce glucose and generate oxygen as a byproduct.

Next, have you ever wondered how the food that we eat is made? Well, the answer is still trees. They use photosynthesis to produce glucose. They turn that glucose into starch and store it in fruits and vegetables.

Trees also give us shelter from the sun's hot, unforgiving rays. They protect the land from soil erosion, fires, flooding and many, many more. They give homes to many birds, animals and insects. They provide us with medicine. And as if all that isn't enough they even help the monsoon rains to reach our country in time.

But, even though trees are helping us in so many ways, there are countless people in this world who still continue to cut them down. We should not be allowing them to do this. It is our duty to protect them. Do they not deserve to live? They have not done any wrong to anyone. All that they have done is sought to help us.

But the trees around us are falling. We should help them to rise again. They have supported us for so long. Now we have to support them. Right now there are still a number of trees left on our planet, or otherwise we would not still be alive but these few are fading fast. It is us, the race of men, who are destroying the world. Treebeard's description of orcs in the Lord of the Rings is an accurate description of those of us who go around destroying everything and anything in our path. “They come with fire! They come with axes! Gnawing, biting, breaking, hacking, burning!! Destroyers and usurpers, CURSE THEM!!”

I know there will always be some who ask 'so, why should we bother to protect trees?' well, if we want to live we must protect them. That is the simple truth. Mother nature is known as such because she gave us life. She has loved us and cared for us but of all her children we are the only ones who show her no gratitude. We do not deserve her love and protection. She has gifted us with life, but in return we are giving her death.

We must protect her. We have to save her. She is growing weak. We have to fight for her survival. She doesn't need buildings or cars. Nor does she need economic development, chemicals or garbage. She needs us to help her to regain her strength and majesty. All that she is asking from us is our love, our care, for her and her other children.

Let's come together to protect mother nature. As Jack Barker said, “Let's stop fighting over who we believe created the planet, & work together against those that choose to destroy it.”
