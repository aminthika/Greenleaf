Stop Saying and Start Doing


The rise of human civilization has left a wake of destruction behind it. We are now reaching the peak of our civilization; therefore the destruction is reaching its point of no return. We are running out of time to make amends.

We need to stop giving out vague metaphoric instructions and take action instead. Don’t tell people to act like their house is on fire; if it were, the first thing anyone would do is abandon it and run. We don't have that option. Don’t tell people they need to ‘save the turtles’ or ‘preserve the rainforests’; show them how, by example.

Plant a tree or sacrifice using that polythene bag which you didn’t even need in the first place. If you start, more will follow. We can’t go on pretending that someone would step up and do the job. We’re all in this together; this is everyone’s planet.

So start small, and start now. You don’t have to grow a thousand trees or even buy a more eco-friendly car. Just stick with the basics - honestly and steadfastly stick to those basic practices - and you’re already halfway there.

We need to step out of the mindset that one tap left running, one fan left turned on won’t really make a difference. But every drop of water is precious and all the carbon dioxide released by your actions add up, and in the end we’re all to blame. The flash floods may not be happening outside your door, the droughts may not have reached you, but they’re happening. And they will keep happening and becoming worse.

We need to act now, while we still can.
